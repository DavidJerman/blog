Japanese is a tough language. It is classified as a Category 5 language by the FSI language difficulty ranking. It shares this spot with Chinese, Cantonese, Japanese, Korean, and Arabic. Difficulty 5 language usually takes about 88 weeks or 2200 hours to reach R-3 proficiency (professional working proficiency)...

And yet can you believe that there are over 150 million speakers worldwide. While Japanese is a tough language, it is learnable and most importantly you can get speaking figure out quite easily. But remember you will have to study every day or you won’t get anywhere. This is after all one of the hardest languages in the world.
